Again

Release year: 2023

Genre: Electronic

Again is the tenth studio album by Oneohtrix Point Never, released on September 29, 2023, via Warp. Lopatin described the album as "a speculative autobiography", drawing comparisons thematically to his 2015 studio album Garden of Delete.
The album artwork features a sculpture created by Norwegian artist Matias Faldbakken and photographed by Vegard Kleven.

Magic Oneohtrix Point Never

Release year: 2020

Genre: Electronic, plunderphonics, pop, sound collage

Magic Oneohtrix Point Never is the ninth studio album by Oneohtrix Point Never, released on October 30, 2020, via Warp. The album draws on a psychedelic radio aesthetic strongly inspired by Magic 106.7, the mondegreen namesake of Lopatin's project, and was recorded during COVID-19 lockdowns, between March and July 2020.
It features collaborations with The Weeknd, Caroline Polachek, Arca and Nolanberollin.
The album cover art was provided by american artist Robert Beatty.

Age Of

Release year: 2018

Genre: Electronic, avant-garde, baroque pop

Age Of is the eighth studio album by Oneohtrix Point Never, released on June 1, 2018, via Warp. Recorded over two years, it is the first Oneohtrix Point Never album to prominently feature Daniel's own vocals.
The album was accompanied by the MYRIAD tour, which premiered as a "conceptual concertscape" in 2018 at the Park Avenue Armory and ended its run in 2019.
It features contributions from James Blake, Anohni, Prurient, Kelsey Lu and Eli Keszler.
The artwork, which employs Jim Shaw's "The Great Whatsit" as a central image, was designed by David Rudnick.

Garden Of Delete

Release year: 2015

Genre: Electronic, nu metal, experimental

Garden of Delete is the seventh studio album by Oneohtrix Point Never, released on November 13, 2015, via Warp. The album was preceded by an enigmatic internet-based promotional campaign, and draws on musical influences such as grunge music, nu metal and popular electronic dance music, as well as themes of adolescence, mutation and abjection.

Rifts

Release year: 2009

Genre: Electronic, hypnagogic, ambient, progressive electronic

Rifts is a 2009 compilation album by Oneohtrix Point Never. The album collects Lopatin's early synth-based recordings dating back to 2003, including the three limited-run LPs Betrayed in the Octagon (2007), Zones Without People (2009) and Russian Mind (2009). It was originally released on No Fun Productions in 2009 as a 2 disc set.

R Plus Seven

Release year: 2013

Genre: Experimental, electronica

R Plus Seven is the sixth studio album by Oneohtrix Point Never, released on September 30, 2013, as his debut album on Warp Records. The album draws heavily on the synthetic sounds of MIDI instruments, 1980s synth presets, and VSTs.
Its release came alongside several collaborations on visual accompaniment with contemporary artists such as Jon Rafman, Takeshi Murata, and Nate Boyce.

The Fall Into Time

Release year: 2013

Genre: Progressive electronic, ambient

The Fall Into Time is a collection of bonus pieces that appeared in Lopatin's Rifts release, released on April 23, 2013, via Warp Records.

Drawn And Quartered

Release year: 2016

Genre: Electronic

Drawn and Quartered is a collection of obscurely released tracks by Lopatin, also found on his Rifts boxset. It was released under his own label Software Recording.

Zones Without People

Release year: 2009

Genre: Ambient

Zones Without People is the second studio album by Oneohtrix Point Never, released on August 6, 2009 via Arbor as a limited-run LP. Most of the material on the album also appeared on the 2009 compilation album Rifts.

Russian Mind

Release year: 2009

Genre: Electronic

Russian Mind is centered around the theme of a Russian cosmonaut past his prime, composing the score to a film from his hospital bed in a room on the east wing of an orbiting infirmary, facing a window overlooking a grove of holographic birch trees.
It was released via Warp on November 1, 2009.

Betrayed in the Octagon

Release year: 2007

Genre: Electronic, new age

Betrayed in the Octagon is the debut studio album by Daniel Lopatin, and the first one to be released under the pseudonym "Oneohtrix Point Never". It was initially released on hand-numbered cassettes on the Deception Island label, later reissued on vinyl in 2009 under No Fun Records.
Songs from the album were later re-released on the 2009 compilation Rifts.

Replica

Release year: 2011

Genre: Electronic, sampledelia, ambient, plunderphonics

Replica is the fifth studio album by Oneohtrix Point Never, released on November 9, 2011, via Mexican Summer and Software. It features co-production with Joel Ford and Al Carlson, and was Lopatin's first work to be recorded in a studio.
The album marks a shift away from Lopatin's previous synth-based works under the alias, instead showcasing a sample-based approach utilizing audio from 1980s and 1990s television advertisements.
